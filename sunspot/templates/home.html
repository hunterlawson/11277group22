What is SunSpot?

Simply put, SunSpot generates accurate regional maps for building solar panels.

As a web application designed to utilize multiple API metrics and recorded data, SunSpot aims to provide the most optimal locations for solar energy farms in the state of Florida. With SunSpot, users can browse through a variety of locations and bookmark for future references. Furthermore, SunSpot also serves as a hub that provides helpful general resources for users of all backgrounds and experience to better understand solar panels.

OpenWeather API

OpenWeather provides both historical and up-to-date weather data for any coordinates in the world.

Solcast API

Solcast focuses on identifying weather forecast data that relates to solar power assets.

Google Maps API

Google Maps assists in creating real-time maps for a variety of purposes.

Who Should Use SunSpot?

SunSpot encourages anyone — personal, municipal, private — to consider utilizing solar energy farms. Users would be able to determine to most suitable areas for building solar farms that would generate the most efficient yield of energy.

Across the state of Florida, there are acres of unused land that could be converted into potential photovoltaic resources. With the help of SunSpot, even residential individuals with large expanses of private property could discern the best locations for personal solar panels.
