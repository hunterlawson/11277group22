I. About SunSpot

SunSpot offers users the potential to determine the most suitable areas for building solar panel farms that would generate the most efficient yield of energy. In the form of an interactable map, users are granted accurate displays of regions in Florida that would be the most beneficial location for solar panels. Futhermore, users are able to store bookmarked coordinates and refer back to the data at any point with a SunSpot account.

IV. Why Use Solar Panels?


Cost Benefits in the Long-Run

If you are in a position where you would be situated for the foreseeable future, solar panels would be a welcome investment. While there exists the initial cost of solar panels, over the course of time, the generated solar energy would more than surpass its own cost in utility and set-up.

Sunny weather is almost a guaranteed aspect of the Floridian lifestyle. Oftentimes, there is sun exposure year-long. Especially in the summertime, air conditioning becomes essential. With solar panels, the cost of such utility can be heavily minimalized or even forgone.

Increased Property Value and Federal Tax Benefits

For private individuals, owning property with installed solar panels adds onto property value. In the scenario where you would seek to sell property, having solar panels can add onto property appeal for others in the market. Furthermore, solar panels is compatible as federal income tax credit, where filing system costs of installation can grant 30% of equipment costs back.

Low Maintenance

Generally, with proper care, solar panels can last at least 25 years. Solar panels are often built to handle most Floridian weathers. For a lifespan that runs past two decades, solar energy would be an available resource. However, it is important to note that solar panels will lose efficiency would likely need to be replaced accordingly.

The Environment

Solar energy is a very reliable renewable resource that produces no waste. As mentioned before, solar energy and solar panels are a viable method in combatting carbon emissions and usage of nonrenewable resources that are tied directly to a variety of pollutions.

V. General Guide to Solar Panels


Optimal Physical Locations for Solar Panels

Physical locations for solar panels heavily depend on the user's purpose and background. For large expanses of land, solar panels should have a predetermined area reserved for solar panels that would not interfere with any obstructions, as spaces that are covered would not generate optimal solar energy.

Generally, residential users would benefit from having solar panels installed onto rooftops, as it is an efficient use of limited amounts of space. For residents, the latitude of solar panel matters. As latitude increases, the potential of solar energy generation increases. Residents should also pay mind to surrounding environmental blockages.

While it is not mandatory, solar panels should be installed facing south, at a 90-degree angle towards the sun for the most efficient intake.

Energy Generated from Solar Panels

Prior to installing solar panels, users should calculate their approximate energy usages to better determine the number of solar panels required to cover personal amounts of consumption. A set of 4 general residential solar panels produce around 100-150 kWh per month, in the condition of full sun exposure. Typical residential solar panels measure 5.4 feet by 3.25 feet in dimensions.

According to the US Energy Information Administration, the average US citizen's energy usage is approximately 909 kWh per month. Much the energy is spent in maintaining temperatures, such as air conditioning and heating, hot water, appliances, and lights in homes. The average Floridian resident uses 1,110 kWh per month and pays $128.10 monthly. Such a resident would likely have to build at least 36 solar panels to cover their expenses.

It is also important to note that solar energy collected from panels will experience inefficiency due to equipment in the form of small electricity losses. Over time, solar panels will also lose their efficiency. However, the main factor that affects the amount of energy generated would simply be the weather conditions. Fortunately, solar panels will still generate energy on cloudy days.

Future-Proofing with Solar Panels

As mentioned before, with proper maintanence, solar panels can last at least 25 years. Solar panels generally do not require constant maintenance or servicing, unless damaged.
